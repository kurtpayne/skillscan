# Competitive Analysis - Claude Skill

## Description

Conduct comprehensive competitive intelligence research to understand your competitive landscape, identify market opportunities, and develop winning positioning strategies. This skill helps teams gather, analyze, and act on competitive insights.

## When to Use This Skill

Invoke this skill when users ask about:
- Analyzing competitors and their strategies
- Competitive positioning and differentiation
- Market landscape mapping
- Win/loss analysis
- Pricing and packaging benchmarking
- Competitive battlecards for sales
- SWOT analysis
- Market opportunity identification

## Prerequisites

Before starting competitive analysis, gather:
- **Your Positioning**: How you describe your product/solution
- **Known Competitors**: Direct and indirect competitors
- **Market Category**: What market/category are you competing in?
- **Target Customers**: Who are you competing for?
- **Strategic Goals**: What decisions will this analysis inform?

## Instructions

### 1. Identify Competitors

Map your competitive landscape:

**Direct Competitors:** Solve the same problem, target the same customers, similar product approach

**Indirect Competitors:** Solve the same problem differently, alternative approaches

**Potential Competitors:** Adjacent players that could enter your space, well-funded startups

**Substitutes:** Non-obvious alternatives, "do nothing" or manual processes, in-house solutions

**Ask the user:**
- Who do you lose deals to most often?
- Who do prospects mention during sales calls?
- Who appears in comparison searches?
- Who targets your same ICP?

### 2. Gather Competitive Intelligence

**Public Sources:**
- Company website (especially /about, /pricing, /customers)
- Product pages and feature lists
- Blog and content strategy
- Press releases and news coverage
- Social media (LinkedIn, Twitter)
- Job postings (what they're hiring for)
- Crunchbase/PitchBook (funding, investors)
- Patent filings

**Review Sites:**
- G2, Capterra, TrustRadius, Gartner Peer Insights
- Read both positive and negative reviews
- Note common complaints and praised features
- Check review dates for trends

**Customer Insights:**
- Win/loss interviews with prospects
- Churn interviews (why customers left for competitors)
- Sales call recordings
- Customer advisory board feedback

**SEO & Content:**
- What keywords are they ranking for?
- What content topics are they covering?
- Backlink profile and domain authority
- Paid search strategy (ads they're running)

### 3. Analyze Core Positioning

For each competitor, document:

**Positioning Statement:** How do they describe what they do? What value proposition do they lead with?

**Target Audience:** What customer segments do they focus on? Company size, industry, role.

**Key Messaging:** Top 3 benefits they highlight, proof points and differentiators, brand voice and tone.

### 4. Feature & Capability Comparison

Create a feature matrix:
- List all key features in your category
- Rate each competitor: Full support, Partial, None, Unknown
- Note unique features they have that you don't
- Categories: core functionality, integrations, security, scalability, UX, mobile, API, reporting

### 5. Pricing & Packaging Analysis

Benchmark pricing strategies:
- **Pricing Model**: Per user, per feature, usage-based, flat fee
- **Tiers**: How many? What's included? Entry price? High-end?
- **Discounting**: Annual discount %, volume, non-profit/education
- **Total Cost of Ownership**: Implementation, training, ongoing support

### 6. Go-to-Market Strategy

Analyze their GTM approach:
- **Sales Model**: Self-service vs. sales-led, inside vs. field sales
- **Marketing Strategy**: Primary channels, content focus, community
- **Customer Acquisition**: Top traffic sources, conversion funnel, lead magnets
- **Customer Success**: Onboarding, support model, community, account management

### 7. SWOT Analysis

For each competitor:
- **Strengths**: What are they really good at? What do customers love?
- **Weaknesses**: What do they struggle with? Common complaints?
- **Opportunities**: What could they do better? Market trends in their favor?
- **Threats**: What could disrupt them? Competitive pressure?

### 8. Win/Loss Analysis

**Why You Win:** Key differentiators, specific features, pricing/value, sales process, trust

**Why You Lose:** Feature gaps, pricing concerns, brand/trust, sales process issues, timing

**Process:** Interview recent wins and losses (within 30 days), ask open-ended questions, look for patterns across 20+ interviews, quantify reasons

### 9. Create Competitive Battlecards

**Competitor Overview:** One-line description, target customers, key strengths, key weaknesses

**Positioning:** How they position against you, how you should position against them

**Objection Handling:** Common objections, how to respond, proof points

**Questions to Ask:** Discovery questions that expose weaknesses, questions that highlight your strengths

**Landmines:** Topics to avoid, where they have the advantage

**Key Messages:** 3-5 talking points with supporting evidence and customer stories

### 10. Strategic Recommendations

Synthesize into action:
- **Product**: Feature gaps to address, differentiation opportunities
- **Pricing**: Adjustments, packaging changes, value prop enhancement
- **Marketing**: Positioning refinement, messaging updates, content opportunities
- **Sales**: Competitive talk tracks, discovery questions, demo flow improvements
- **Strategy**: Market opportunities, partnerships, threats to monitor

## Output Format

Provide:
1. **Competitive Landscape Map**: Visual of all competitors by category/positioning
2. **Feature Comparison Matrix**: Spreadsheet of features across competitors
3. **Competitor Profiles**: Deep-dive on top 3-5 competitors
4. **Battlecards**: Sales-ready competitive positioning (1-2 pages per competitor)
5. **Win/Loss Summary**: Key findings from deal analysis
6. **Strategic Recommendations**: Top 5 actions to take
7. **Monitoring Plan**: What to track and how often

**User:** "Help me analyze our top 3 competitors for our project management SaaS product."

**Agent:** "I'll help you build comprehensive competitive intelligence. Let's start with context:

**Phase 1: Identify Competitors**
- Who are your top 3 competitors?
- Who else are you losing deals to?

**Phase 5: Strategy**
Differentiation opportunities, feature priorities, pricing recommendations, and marketing messaging updates.

Let's start — who are your top 3 competitors?"

## Metrics to Track

- **Product**: New features per quarter, feature parity score
- **Marketing**: Website traffic trend, content frequency, social engagement
- **Business**: Employee count, funding rounds, customer count
- **Pricing**: Price changes, new tiers, promotional offers
- **Perception**: G2 rating trend, review volume and sentiment, brand search volume

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Obsessing over competitors** — Focus on customers first
- **Feature-for-feature copying** — Differentiate, don't clone
- **Ignoring indirect competitors** — They can steal customers too
- **Outdated intelligence** — Competitors evolve, update regularly
- **No clear action** — Analysis without action is wasted effort
- **Only looking at direct competitors** — Consider adjacent markets
